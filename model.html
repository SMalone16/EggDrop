Prototype Like a Professional

Follow these modeling moves inspired by aerospace and automotive engineers.

Sketch with Purpose

  • Draw your design from at least two angles and label each part.
  • Use arrows to show where forces will travel through the structure.
  • Color-code cushioning, frame, and parachute elements.

Plan Materials

  • List the Launch Bucks cost beside each material.
  • Note which materials are reusable for second iterations.
  • Match each material to its job (cushion, slow, support, secure).

Build & Iterate

Modeling Checklist

  • Measure twice before cutting or taping materials.
  • Keep an organized workstation with labeled bins.
  • Construct in layers: frame first, cushioning second, exterior last.
  • Gently shake the model to check for rattles or weak joints.
  • Log each change in a design notebook or digital journal.

Engineering Practices

  • Conduct quick bench tests: drop a marble or plastic egg to test cushioning.
  • Create a control drawing so revisions can be compared.
  • Use design sprints: focus on one improvement for 10 minutes, then review.
  • Hold a peer design review and invite feedback.

Focus on Critical Features

Cushioning Core

Design an inner cradle that hugs the egg. Use soft materials that compress slowly, like cotton or foam. Add crossing strips of tape to hold the egg gently without squeezing.

Structure & Frame

Build triangles and cross-braces, just like engineers strengthen bridges. Make sure the egg is centered so the weight is balanced.

Drag Devices

Increase surface area with parachutes, streamers, or wings. Smooth out wrinkles so air flows evenly and prevents spinning.
