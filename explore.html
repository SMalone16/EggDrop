Key Concepts for Egg Drop Engineers

Physics Focus

  • Gravity pulls the egg downward and gives it speed.
  • Air resistance pushes up on wide, light designs to slow the fall.
  • Impact force is the sudden push when the egg meets the ground.
  • Energy transfer moves kinetic energy into cushions, crumple zones, or springs.
  • Momentum tells us heavier objects take longer to stop, so we need gradual slowing.

Engineering Moves

  • Create crumple zones that squish and absorb energy.
  • Use suspension systems (rubber bands, straws) to hold the egg away from outer walls.
  • Balance mass and drag for a gentle landing.
  • Prototype quickly and take notes on what works.

Challenge Guidelines

Criteria for Success

  • The egg shell stays uncracked after the final drop.
  • The device uses no more than 25 Launch Bucks in materials.
  • The design lands within the marked drop zone.
  • The team can explain how physics informed their choices.

Rules to Follow

  • Devices must be built during class time using approved supplies.
  • No parachutes wider than 40 centimeters.
  • Eggs remain in their protective cases between trials.
  • Clean up all materials and reset the drop zone after each test.
